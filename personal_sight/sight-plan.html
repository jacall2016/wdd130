Overview

Celestial habitability

Ever wondered if your day lasted for a week? How about having a year that lasted longer than a lifetime? This is what life around some celestial objects may be like. In this guide, we explore the various fantastical environments space cooks up for us and how civilizations past and modern would thrive under these strange exotic conditions. For example, our sun has a tremendous effect on life in our solar system. Get too close and your planet may end up a boiling stew, like venus. Too far away and it may never thaw out, like Mars. Spin too fast and your world may be engulfed in constant storms, spin too slowly and one half may burn while the other side freezes. The recipe for making a world is precise, but often life at the extremes is the most interesting.

Audience

The intended audience for this websight are science inthusiests. It is intended as a sight to springboard creative ideas about what life may be like in the cosmos. It is focused on those with an intrest in astronomy, astrobiology, spectlive biology, astrophysics, orbital dynamics and other reated space sciences. It also ties in elements from Anthropology and the study of speclitive alien sosieties and the effect of diffiecnt habitable systems on the development of sentient civilizations.
